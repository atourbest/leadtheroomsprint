The Program

How does Lead the Room work?

Thirty years of leading rooms taught us it comes down to four things. You build CONFIDENCE to own the room, sharpen SKILLS to influence it, master ENERGY to read and move it, and use DESIGN to shape it — one lab per skill, two weeks, every session practised live.

Here's what Lead the Room is NOT:

  • ✕It's NOT a generic facilitation course full of tips and techniques you've already heard a hundred times before.
  • ✕It's NOT a self-paced video library where you watch alone, hope something sticks, and never actually practise in a real room.
  • ✕It's NOT built for experienced facilitators or leaders with positional authority.

The Opportunity

Why learn to lead the room?

01

Influence Without Authority

Leadership no longer comes with a reporting line. The real work happens across teams — aligning peers, stakeholders, and seniors you can't instruct — and the room is where that work gets done.

02

Presence That Gets Noticed

The room is where reputations are made. Lead a meeting, town hall, or off-site well and the right people see a leader — not the person who holds the agenda.

03

Outcomes You Can Point To

Most meetings end in more meetings. When you reliably end in decisions, alignment, and momentum, you become the person trusted with the moments that matter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Still wondering?

How much time do I need?+
Two 90-minute live labs per week for two weeks — four sessions in total. The only homework is using the tools in the flow of work.
What if I can't attend live?+
Every session has recordings and other videos you can access immediately, so you can catch up before the next lab. That said, the labs are where the action happens — attend live whenever you can.
I'm not naturally confident in front of a room. Can I still do this?+
That's exactly what the first session is for. Confidence in a room is a practice, not a personality trait — and you'll leave with a repeatable loop for building it.
Do I need a leadership title or direct reports?+
No. Lead the Room is built for people who lead with influence rather than authority — chiefs of staff, program leads, experts, and managers who need to move rooms they don't command.
Is this just facilitation training?+
No — it's a leadership method. You'll pick up facilitation techniques along the way, but the outcome is bigger: leading any room toward the result you need.
How is this different from presentation-skills courses?+
Presentation training teaches you to deliver polished content at a room. Lead the Room teaches you to work with the room — reading it, moving it, and reaching outcomes together.
How long do I have access?+
You keep lifetime access to the session videos and resources, plus ongoing access to the community — and the Field Guide is yours on your shelf.
Is there a guarantee?+
Yes. Join the first lab, and if it's not right for you, let us know within 48 hours for a full refund — no questions asked.
